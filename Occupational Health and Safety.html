Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Course

Course Description

The Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Course is designed to equip participants with the essential knowledge and skills needed to create and maintain a safe working environment. The course focuses on understanding the principles of workplace safety, identifying potential hazards, and implementing effective risk management strategies. Whether you are a manager, supervisor, or employee, this course provides practical insights into ensuring compliance with safety regulations and fostering a culture of safety in your organization.

Objectives

Provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of occupational health and safety principles.
Develop skills in hazard identification, risk assessment, and implementation of safety measures.
Equip participants with the tools needed to create and maintain a safe working environment.

FIntroduction to Occupational Health and Safety

OHS Principles: Understanding the core concepts and importance of occupational health and safety.
Legal Framework: Overview of laws and regulations governing workplace safety.
Roles and Responsibilities: Defining the roles of employers, employees, and safety officers in maintaining a safe workplace.

Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

Recognizing Hazards: Techniques for identifying physical, chemical, biological, and ergonomic hazards.
Risk Assessment Methods: How to assess the level of risk associated with identified hazards. .
Control Measures: Implementing effective control measures to minimize or eliminate risks.

Workplace Safety Practices

Safe Work Procedures: Developing and implementing safe work practices and standard operating procedures (SOPs).
Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Proper selection, use, and maintenance of PPE.
Emergency Preparedness: Creating and maintaining emergency response plans, including fire safety and evacuation procedures.

Health and Safety Management Systems

Developing an OHS Management System: How to create and implement an effective OHS management system.
Continuous Improvement: Techniques for monitoring, reviewing, and improving workplace safety practices.
Safety Audits and Inspections: Conducting regular safety audits and inspections to ensure compliance.

Training and Communication

Safety Training Programs: Designing and delivering effective OHS training for employees at all levels.
Communication Techniques: Ensuring clear and effective communication of safety policies and procedures.
Building a Safety Culture: Fostering a proactive safety culture where employees are engaged and motivated to prioritize safety.
